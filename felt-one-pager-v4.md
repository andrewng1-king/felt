## Executive Summary

Leaders always want to know what their people really think — but employees can't say it directly. Not because they don't want to. Because they can't afford to.

felt. is the trusted intermediary. It reads what happened in your most important internal conversations — voice, tone, language — and tells you what your employees felt but couldn't say to your face. Not what you think happened. What actually did.

It's built for one thing only: the conversations that decide if your best people leave or stay.

## The Problem

Leaders want to know what their people really think. Employees want to tell them. But they can't — because the power dynamic makes it unsafe.

So the manager walks out of the conversation guessing. The employee walks out holding back. And slowly, quietly, the trust erodes.

- HR tools measure the damage after someone quits. Nobody prevents the conversation that caused it.
- Human coaching costs $300–500/month, is slow, and doesn't scale to every hard 1:1.
- No tool gives a manager the feedback their employee couldn't safely give them.

**The core pain:** Your best people are forming opinions about you after every conversation. You have no way to know what those opinions are — until they hand in their resignation.

**Why employees can't tell you:** They want to. But they can't afford to. You're their manager. They need their job. So they stay quiet, disengage slowly, and eventually leave. And you never knew what conversation started it.

## Solution

**felt.** reads the signals in your most important internal conversations — voice tone, language patterns, and transcript — and tells you what you couldn't see in the room.

**Pre-conversation**
- Role-play the hard talk with AI before it happens
- Stress-test your phrasing, detect emotionally loaded language
- Pre-Talk Readiness score: are you actually ready for this conversation?

**Post-conversation**
- Empathy Mirror: how the other person likely felt
- Full Conversation Intelligence breakdown across 11 metrics
- The specific moment things shifted — and exactly what you said that caused it

**Longitudinal**
- Behavioral Trends: your blind spots, tracked over time
- Risk Signals: is trust eroding in this relationship?
- Relationship memory: how this specific person is changing across conversations

**How a conversation gets flagged as important:** Not by the manager. felt. auto-analyzes every logged conversation once connected to Fathom/Fireflies. Importance is surfaced after the fact — a Risk Signal spike or an Empathy Mirror drop is what tells the manager "this one mattered," not a manual tag applied beforehand. Asking a manager to pre-judge importance repeats the same blind spot felt. is built to catch.

## What Makes felt. Different

**The Empathy Mirror is the only metric in the market that analyzes the other person — not the manager.** Every other tool (Kona, 15Five, Gong) tells you how *you* performed. felt. tells you how *they felt*. That's a different product. That's a different category.

**Multimodal analysis.** felt. reads voice tone + speech patterns + transcript together. Not just what was said — how it was said, and what it triggered. This is what makes the inference accurate, not just suggestive.

**Built for internal conversations only.** Not sales calls (that's Gong). Not team standups. Not hiring, firing, or comp conversations — different rubrics, deliberately out of scope. The specific, high-stakes conversations between a manager and someone who matters to them.

## Revenue Model & Pricing

**Model:** B2B SaaS, per seat, self-serve/founder-led entry motion (not B2C — there's no consumer version of this product).

Tiers are differentiated by what the product does, not just by who's buying:

- **Individual — $25/month.** Post-conversation analysis only: full Empathy Mirror + core metric suite, unlimited transcripts, one aggregate benchmark stat. Self-serve, manager expenses it, no approval needed. The "prove it to yourself" tier.
- **Pro/Team — $45/seat/month.** Everything in Individual, plus Pre-conversation prep (role-play, Pre-Talk Readiness score) and an anonymized org-level Risk Signal dashboard for the Head of People — flags which manager relationships need support, no employee-level data ever shown. This is the tier that turns felt. from a personal tool into an org-wide signal, and where ARR compounds.
- **Executive — $75–100/month.** Not higher volume — deeper tracking. Execs have fewer conversations but each is higher stakes, so this tier extends relationship memory and analysis depth for a small number of named, critical relationships (co-founder, key exec, board), plus priority support.

**Pilot approach:** "Proof of Signal" — 2 weeks, paid flat fee ($200–300), pre-agreed success metric. Not a 90-day free pilot. Pilot fee is credited toward the first month if the design partner continues to a paid seat.

**Entry motion:** Individual self-serves at $25 → word of mouth inside org → Head of People formalizes at Pro/Team tier

**Year 1 (Validation Phase):** 10 design partners → 5 paying accounts → ~$15K ARR. Year 1 is not a revenue phase — it's a signal phase. Success is an undeniable first insight, not a revenue number.

**Year 2 (Distribution):** Plugin live on Fathom/Fireflies, bottom-up motion kicks in → ~50 paying accounts → ~$150K ARR

**Year 3 (Scale):** Head of People motion formalized, Pro/Team tier compounds → ~150+ accounts → $600K–$1M ARR

**Long-term:** Behavioral data compounds per user. Switching cost grows with every conversation logged.

## Go-To-Market Strategy

**Bottom-up, not top-down.**

The person who feels the pain is the manager — not HR, not L&D. Managers self-select in, expense the subscription, and tell colleagues. L&D enters to formalize adoption that's already happening, not to evaluate a pilot.

**Phase 1 — Design Partners (Month 1–3)**
- 10 managers and founders from existing network (Telestar and similar)
- Target: Founders/CEOs and Heads of People at 20–200 person companies — fast decisions, direct pain
- Obsess over the first 5-minute experience. First insight after first conversation must be undeniable.
- No engineering until the manual version works.

**Phase 2 — Plugin Distribution + Bottom-Up (Month 3–9)**
- Integrate with Fathom and Fireflies as a plugin layer
- Position: "The Conversation Intelligence brain for your meeting transcripts"
- Individual managers self-serve at $15–30/month → expense card → no approval needed
- Word of mouth drives intra-company spread

**Phase 3 — Direct B2B, Heads of People (Month 9+)**
- Sell to Heads of People and CPOs at scale-ups — they see adoption already happening in their org
- Anchor to team engagement sub-scores (Culture Amp, Lattice) — metric they already track
- Introduce felt.'s own benchmarks as the standard for manager communication quality

**Phase 4 — Enterprise L&D (When ready)**
- Requires L&D-fluent hire or co-founder on team first
- Enter with case studies, adoption data, and felt. benchmark credibility already established

## Top 3 Risks & Mitigations

**1. Habit formation**
- Risk: Managers open it 3 times and stop
- Mitigation: felt. auto-analyzes every logged conversation once connected (Fathom/Fireflies) — no manual trigger, no manager decision required. Importance is surfaced by the product after the fact (Risk Signal spike, Empathy Mirror drop), not self-reported beforehand. First session delivers undeniable insight in under 5 minutes.

**2. Audio / Legal consent** 🔴 UNRESOLVED
- Risk: Voice analysis requires audio access. Many US states require two-party consent. GDPR in EU applies to biometric/voice data.
- Options: Via Fathom/Fireflies API (cleanest), self-record with in-app consent, built-in felt. recorder with explicit consent flow
- Must be resolved before go-to-market

**3. Microsoft / Kona closes the gap**
- Risk: Kona (15Five) already in the market. Microsoft Copilot could add an EQ layer.
- Mitigation: Go deeper on empathy mirror and multimodal analysis than either will care to. felt. builds for the individual. They build for scale. Be too personal to ignore, too niche to prioritize.

## Design Partner Landing Page Guidance

This page has one audience: design partners, not investors. One primary ask, one secondary note.

- **Primary CTA: the pilot.** "Proof of Signal" — 2-week paid pilot, flat fee ($200–300), one pre-agreed success metric. This is the thing you're asking them to say yes to.
- **Secondary note: post-pilot expectations, as an estimate — not a live price.** State clearly it's provisional: "Estimated pricing after the pilot: $25–45/seat/month, depending on team size — final numbers set based on what we learn together." Don't present it as a fixed, current price.
- **Show one range, not the full tier table.** Design partners at this stage are Individual or Team buyers. Leave the Executive tier off this page — it's for a different persona and adds noise here.
- **Tie the pilot fee to the subscription.** One line: "Your pilot fee is credited toward your first month if you continue." Removes the awkwardness of two separate charges and makes continuing feel like one relationship, not a new sales pitch.
